The Experience

Where Glamour Anchors
in Ancient Waters

Bodrum commands the southwestern tip of the Aegean coast with the confidence of a place that knows exactly what it is — irresistibly beautiful, deeply historic, and completely intoxicating.

The whitewashed town tumbles down to a harbour guarded by the magnificent Castle of St. Peter, its twin bays framing views that have inspired poets and painters for centuries. By day, the Aegean shimmers in colours that shift from aquamarine to deepest sapphire as private gulets thread between hidden coves.

By evening, Bodrum transforms into one of the eastern Mediterranean's most sophisticated social scenes — rooftop bars, candlelit courtyard restaurants, and a marina alive with the gentle clink of mooring lines and the murmur of many languages.

Ibtiseme Tour curates Bodrum at its finest — private yacht charters to secluded bays, boutique hotel reservations at the peninsula's most coveted addresses, and VIP access to experiences that the ordinary visitor cannot reach.

Unmissable Experiences

What Awaits
in Bodrum

Private Yacht Charters

Commission your own gulet and chart a course through the Bodrum Peninsula's most spectacular bays — anchoring in crystalline coves accessible only by sea, dining on deck as the stars multiply overhead.

Castle of St. Peter

Built by the Knights Hospitaller in the 15th century from the stones of the Mausoleum of Halicarnassus — one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World — this fortress dominates Bodrum's harbour with timeless authority.

Vibrant Bodrum Marina

The marina is Bodrum's beating heart — a promenade of superyachts and traditional gulets, waterfront restaurants serving Aegean mezze, and an atmosphere of effortless, sun-soaked sophistication.

Luxury Boutique Hotels

From clifftop retreats with infinity pools suspended above the Aegean to intimate whitewashed villas hidden in olive groves, Bodrum's accommodation offers some of the most desirable luxury stays in all of Turkey.

Aegean Cuisine & Nightlife

Fresh octopus grilled over charcoal, Aegean herb salads, olive oil pressed from century-old trees, and meze that unfolds course by course over long golden evenings — Bodrum's culinary culture is a destination in itself.

Day Trips to Nearby Islands

The Greek island of Kos lies minutes away by speedboat. Karada Island's thermal springs bubble up beneath the sea. And countless uninhabited Aegean islets await those who want to feel, for a few hours, utterly alone in paradise.
