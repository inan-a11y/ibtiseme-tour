The Experience

Riviera Luxury Meets
Ancient Grandeur

Antalya is Turkey's jewel on the Mediterranean — a city where Roman triumphal arches stand beside boutique harbour restaurants, and five-star resort pools overlook the same cerulean sea that ancient civilisations once sailed across.

The old town of Kaleiçi winds through Roman walls, Ottoman mansions, and Byzantine churches, each street a chapter in a history stretching back over two millennia. Beyond the old city, Antalya's coastline unfolds in a series of spectacular bays — from the iconic pebble beaches of Lara and Konyaaltı to secluded coves accessible only by private boat.

The region's world-class resorts offer every conceivable luxury, while the surrounding mountains provide dramatic backdrops for those who want to venture beyond the shore.

Ibtiseme Tour arranges your Antalya experience with the precision and care it deserves — private airport transfers, exclusive resort reservations, guided historical tours, and bespoke blue voyage itineraries.

Unmissable Experiences

What Awaits
in Antalya

Crystal Mediterranean Beaches

Sink into the shores of Lara Beach, Konyaaltı, or Kaputaş — where the sea glows turquoise and the water is so clear you can trace the outlines of ancient amphora on the seabed below.

Kaleiçi Old Town

Step inside Roman walls into a labyrinth of Ottoman-era mansions, Byzantine ruins, and narrow stone streets alive with the fragrance of jasmine. One of Turkey's most beautifully preserved historic quarters.

Ancient Roman Ruins

Perge, Aspendos, and Termessos stand as monuments to Rome's imperial reach. The theatre at Aspendos remains so well preserved it still hosts international opera performances beneath open skies.

Luxury Resort Life

Antalya hosts some of the Mediterranean's most spectacular five-star resorts — sprawling properties with private beaches, infinity pools, world-class spas, and culinary experiences that rival any capital city.

Boat Trips & Blue Voyages

Charter a private gulet and sail the turquoise bays, anchor in secluded coves inaccessible by road, and watch the sun set behind limestone cliffs while the cook prepares fresh catch on deck.

Düden Waterfalls

The Lower Düden Waterfall plunges directly into the sea from a cliff face — a sight of extraordinary natural drama. Accessible by boat for a perspective you will remember for a lifetime.
